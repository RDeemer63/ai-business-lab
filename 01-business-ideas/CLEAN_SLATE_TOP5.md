## What This Pass Found

The top-scoring ideas are not about Ryan's expertise. They are compliance, automation, and document-generation tools that score high because the underlying business mechanics are strong regardless of who builds them. The top 5 cluster around a consistent pattern: regulated markets where businesses face real legal consequences for inaction and where AI can automate the detection and reporting work at near-zero marginal cost.

The highest-scoring ideas from Track B are more passive than the highest-scoring ideas from Track A. The trade-off is that Ryan's existing distribution does not help him sell them as quickly. That gap is real and worth knowing.

## The Five Finalists

### Finalist 1 — Automated Job Description Bias Checker
**Business quality score: 54.5 / 60**
**Ryan fit score: 11 / 20**

A self-serve SaaS tool that analyzes job descriptions for biased language — gendered words, unnecessary experience requirements, exclusionary phrasing — and suggests rewrites. Fully automated: user pastes text, LLM returns analysis, result displayed instantly. No human review, no ongoing delivery, no client relationship. Subscription model at $49-199/month.

This is the highest-scoring idea by business fundamentals because it combines five rare properties: near-instant speed to first dollar (free tool launch on ProductHunt), zero marginal cost per analysis, self-serve conversion, no customer service burden, and zero founder dependency in delivery. Competition from Textio and Gender Decoder is real but both are enterprise-priced. The affordable self-serve tier is genuinely open.

The Ryan fit score of 11/20 reflects reality: HR tech is not Ryan's world, and his existing audience is contractors not HR managers. There is no distribution shortcut. Growth depends on SEO and product-led virality, not existing relationships. This is a strong business that Ryan would be building from scratch with no head start.

### Finalist 2 — ADA Website Compliance Monitoring
**Business quality score: 54 / 60**
**Ryan fit score: 15 / 20**

Automated monthly scan of client websites for WCAG 2.1 accessibility violations. Report delivered as a PDF showing every issue, severity level, and fix description in plain English. $97-197/month per client or site portfolio. The primary acquisition channel is cold email with a free unsolicited scan as the hook.

ADA demand letters are real. Law firms send hundreds per day targeting websites with accessibility violations. A business owner who receives one pays $5,000-50,000 to resolve it. A business owner who avoids one pays $97/month. The fear-driven buying dynamic makes this the easiest sell of the top 5.

Ryan fit is 15/20 — the highest of the top 5 — because the sales motion (cold email to SMBs with a free scan result) is something Ryan can execute immediately using existing contacts. ADA compliance is learnable in a week. The technical pipeline (accessibility scan API plus report generator) is within Ryan's technical capabilities.

The largest risk is cold email deliverability: the hook requires sending unsolicited emails with a scan result, which requires careful domain reputation management.

### Finalist 3 — Privacy Policy & Cookie Consent Monitor
**Business quality score: 54 / 60**
**Ryan fit score: 15 / 20**

Automated monthly scan of client websites for GDPR and CCPA compliance gaps: missing or outdated privacy policy, non-compliant cookie consent banner, undisclosed data collection practices. Same mechanics as Finalist 2 — automated scan, PDF report, monthly subscription at $97-197/month.

This idea and Finalist 2 are complementary. The same pipeline, the same cold email hook, the same client profile. A natural pairing is to offer both as a bundle — ADA compliance plus privacy compliance — at $147-247/month. That bundle has a stronger value proposition than either alone and reduces the sales effort per client.

Ryan fit is 15/20 for the same reasons as Finalist 2. The difference between them is market urgency: ADA demand letters are more common and more feared than CCPA enforcement actions against small US businesses. Finalist 2 is the stronger standalone; Finalist 3 is the stronger upsell.

### Finalist 4 — Automated Contract Redline Summarizer
**Business quality score: 53.5 / 60**
**Ryan fit score: 14 / 20**

Self-serve SaaS that takes any contract PDF and returns a plain-English summary: key obligations, payment terms, termination rights, liability exposure, and flagged non-standard clauses. Target: founders, freelancers, and small business owners who receive vendor contracts and don't want to pay $500+ for a lawyer to read them. $49-99/month subscription or $9 per-contract one-time.

The painful problem is universal: every business that signs contracts feels it. The self-serve model means no sales calls, no ongoing client relationship, no customization. The product works or it doesn't — which is both the risk and the appeal.

Ryan fit is 14/20. The technical build (PDF extraction plus clause analysis) is within Ryan's capabilities. The challenge is distribution: reaching founders and small business owners through SEO content is slower than reaching a known audience through existing relationships.

The largest hidden bottleneck is PDF extraction quality. Real-world contracts come in formats that break text extraction — scanned documents, multi-column layouts, handwritten amendments. Handling edge cases takes longer than building the core product.

### Finalist 5 — SOC 2 Evidence Collection Template Pack
**Business quality score: 53.5 / 60**
**Ryan fit score: 11 / 20**

A complete, auditor-tested template pack for startups preparing their first SOC 2 Type II audit. Delivered as a Google Drive folder or Notion workspace containing all policy templates, evidence collection spreadsheets, and an audit prep guide. One-time purchase at $497-797. Zero ongoing work after initial build.

This scores 53.5 because the business mechanics are nearly perfect: digital product, 100% margin, self-serve checkout, no client relationship, no customer service. The path to $5k/month is 10 sales — a very low bar. The problem (SOC 2 audit preparation) is universally painful and well-understood by the buyer.

Ryan fit is 11/20. The honest blocker: building this product requires genuine SOC 2 domain expertise. An inaccurate template pack causes real damage — startups rely on it in actual audits. Ryan would need either 80-100 hours of research to become authoritative enough, or a content partner with compliance experience. Neither is fast. This is not a product Ryan can build and launch in 30 days without compromising quality.

## Core Trade-Off for Each Finalist

**Job Description Bias Checker:** Best business score, worst Ryan fit. Fully automated, self-serve, no client relationships. But zero distribution advantage and a competitive market. Growth requires SEO or paid acquisition — neither of which leverages Ryan's existing assets.

**ADA Website Compliance Monitoring:** Highest Ryan fit of the top 5. The cold email hook (free scan result) is executable immediately using existing SMB contacts. The fear-driven buying dynamic is the strongest conversion mechanism of any idea in the top 10. The blocker is cold email deliverability, which requires careful setup.

**Privacy Policy Monitor:** Strongest as an add-on to ADA monitoring rather than a standalone product. The same pipeline, the same clients, the same sales motion. Bundled with Finalist 2, it becomes a $147-247/month "website legal compliance monitor" with a stronger value proposition than either alone.

**Contract Redline Summarizer:** The most universal problem on the list — every business that signs contracts feels this pain. The self-serve model is strong. The challenge is that PDF extraction edge cases require more engineering work than the core product, and the SEO path to customers is slower than Ryan's existing relationship-based channels.

**SOC 2 Template Pack:** Best business mechanics of the five for an operator who wants zero ongoing work. But the prerequisite domain expertise is real and cannot be skipped. This is not a first product for someone without SOC 2 background.

## Bundle Opportunity

**Finalists 2 and 3 are a natural pair.** ADA compliance monitoring plus privacy compliance monitoring, offered together at $147-247/month as "Website Legal Compliance Monitor." Same client, same pipeline, same acquisition motion, two separate reports. This bundle is more defensible than either product sold separately because clients who cancel one are less likely to cancel both.

**Finalist 1 (bias checker) is a standalone product with a distinct buyer.** HR managers are not the same audience as the SMB owners who would buy ADA or privacy monitoring. No natural bundling opportunity with the other four finalists.

Track B ideas uniformly score higher on "risk of becoming another job":

Track A ideas score 2-4 on that metric. The Local SEO Subscription scored 2 — meaning it carries genuine risk of becoming a traditional agency relationship.

That gap is the most important finding from this research pass.
