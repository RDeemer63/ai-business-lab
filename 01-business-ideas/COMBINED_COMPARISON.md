## Scoring Note

Track A used 10 criteria including founder advantage (C8), which inflates scores for ideas where Ryan's existing assets are the primary differentiator. Track B excluded founder advantage entirely. To compare across tracks, this document uses six normalized metrics:

**Original metrics:**
- **Business quality (1-5):** How strong is this business model if stripped of who is building it?
- **Ryan fit (1-5):** How much does Ryan's existing position accelerate this specific idea?
- **Job risk (1-5):** How likely is this business to require 20+ hours/week from Ryan after 12 months? (5 = very unlikely to become a job; 1 = this is a job)

**Venture Studio metrics (added 2026-07-10, per Chief Architect recommendation):**
- **Strategic leverage (1-5):** Does success here make the studio stronger — improving other ventures, building platform capabilities, or expanding distribution? (5 = platform capability; 1 = standalone only)
- **Reusable capability (1-5):** Will this experiment produce workflows, agents, or technology useful in future experiments? (5 = high reuse; 1 = one-off)
- **Portfolio synergy (1-5):** Does this strengthen existing Ryan ventures (Tulboxx, Throttled Up, Outta the Weeds) or stand entirely alone? (5 = direct synergy; 1 = no connection)

These three metrics answer a different question than revenue potential. They measure what the Venture Studio learns and retains — independent of whether the business succeeds.

## Where the Tracks Agree

**Both tracks agree that fully automated, recurring-subscription services are the target.** Every top-5 idea in both tracks is recurring and AI-delivered. The instinct is correct: the business model is right.

**Both tracks agree that one-time purchases are weaker long-term.** The SOC 2 template pack and the OSHA document generator score lower on recurring revenue. The newsletter and video script ideas in Track A have the same problem. Single purchases are fine for cash flow; they are not the path to stable $10k/month.

**Both tracks agree that anything requiring custom client work creates job risk.** The Local SEO Subscription scored lowest on job risk in Track A because the client relationship is ongoing, complex, and reputation-dependent. This is consistent with the skepticism rule applied across both tracks.

## Where the Tracks Diverge

**Business quality.** Track B ideas score materially higher on business quality when founder advantage is removed. The Track A ideas depend on Ryan's existing platform and relationships to reach their scores. Without those assets, Track A ideas are solidly average businesses competing in crowded markets. Track B ideas are strong businesses regardless of who builds them.

**Job risk.** Track B ideas score uniformly better on job risk. All five Track B finalists can operate under 2 hours/week post-stabilization. Track A ideas carry meaningful ongoing client communication — especially the Local SEO Subscription — which creates structural risk of reverting to a service business.

**Speed to first dollar.** Track A wins here. Ryan can close the first content subscription client this week by calling someone he knows. Track B requires either product builds or cold outreach to audiences Ryan doesn't have relationships with. That gap is 2-6 weeks for most Track B ideas.

**Distribution advantage.** Track A ideas use Ryan's existing contractor network as the primary acquisition channel. Track B ideas require building a new distribution channel from scratch — SEO, cold email, ProductHunt, or paid acquisition. That is not a fatal flaw, but it is real work that Track A skips.

## Cross-Track Verdict

The two tracks reveal a clear tension: Ryan's existing assets create a head start, but they also create a ceiling.

Track A ideas are faster to revenue because Ryan's contractor network and existing platform reduce friction. But they compete in markets (local SEO, AI content, marketing services) where the business quality without Ryan's personal involvement is moderate. The Local SEO Subscription's job risk score of 2/5 is the number worth staring at: it is the idea where the path from "I have 15 clients" to "I have a job" is shortest.

Track B ideas are stronger as durable businesses. They are systematically more automated, more passive, and less dependent on any specific person's reputation. The ADA compliance monitoring idea scores 4.5/5 on business quality and 5/5 on job risk — those numbers are better than any Track A idea on both dimensions. The trade-off is that Ryan builds these with no distribution head start.

The question underneath this is: what does Ryan actually want?

If the goal is fastest path to $5k/month, Track A wins — specifically, the AI Content Subscription using the existing Throttled Up Platform.

If the goal is a business that does not require Ryan to be involved to deliver value, Track B wins — specifically, ADA compliance monitoring or the privacy policy monitor.

If the goal is both — fast revenue now, passive business long-term — the answer is to validate Track A first (because it's faster), then build Track B alongside it (because it's better).

## Recommended Validation Sequence

These three tests can run simultaneously. Each takes less than a day to set up and produces a real signal within 7 days. Total cost: zero.

### Test 1 — AI Content Subscription (Track A)
**Why first:** Fastest possible validation using existing assets. Ryan can call contractors today.
**Test:** Post in 3 contractor-focused Facebook groups. Offer 30-day free trial in exchange for testimonial. Count: how many contractors reply, how many want a call, how many convert.
**Signal:** If 5+ contractors express genuine interest, the demand is real. If nobody responds, the positioning or the audience is wrong.
**Time to signal:** 3-5 days.

### Test 2 — ADA Compliance Monitoring (Track B)
**Why second:** Highest Ryan fit of Track B ideas. Free scan hook is completely automated. Can run simultaneously with Test 1.
**Test:** Run free aXe scans on 20 local business websites. Write a one-paragraph plain-English summary of their worst violations. Send a cold email with the summary and a PDF. Count: open rate, reply rate, and how many ask about ongoing monitoring.
**Signal:** If 3+ out of 20 reply with interest, the hook is working. If zero reply, the problem is either the email or the fear isn't strong enough in this market segment.
**Time to signal:** 5-7 days.

### Test 3 — AI Lead Follow-Up Service (Track A)
**Why third:** Solves the most viscerally painful problem for contractors. Validates whether urgency around missed leads is the buying trigger Ryan thinks it is.
**Test:** Build a simple GHL demo with one AI conversation flow for an HVAC contractor. Walk 3 contractors through a live demo. Ask: "Would you pay $297/month for this?" Count: how many say yes and whether any would pay before you build the full system.
**Time to signal:** 5-7 days (requires building the demo first).

## Honest Answer: Inside or Outside?

The best business opportunities for Ryan are partially inside his expertise and partially outside it.

Inside: Ryan's contractor network and the Throttled Up Platform give him a real head start on Track A ideas. That head start is worth using. The AI Content Subscription and AI Lead Follow-Up Service are genuinely good businesses made faster by Ryan's existing position. Ignoring that advantage would be wasteful.

Outside: The best businesses by pure quality metrics — ADA compliance monitoring, privacy monitoring, contract summarization — have no natural connection to Ryan's existing work. They are better businesses on the fundamentals (lower job risk, more passive, more defensible) but require Ryan to build new distribution from scratch.

The practical answer: start with Track A to generate revenue quickly, then allocate 20% of time to building one Track B idea in parallel. The ADA compliance monitor is the strongest Track B candidate for Ryan specifically because the SMB sales motion overlaps enough with his existing work to reduce the distribution gap. The two businesses do not compete with each other, do not confuse the same audience, and can run simultaneously without meaningful coordination cost.

Do not wait for Ryan to choose one track. The validation tests above cost nothing and take one week. Run them first, then choose.
