## The Short Version

Two of the top five ideas are the same platform. Three of the five can be combined. The core choice is between speed and ceiling: launch the content subscription in weeks on what is already built, or spend 4-8 weeks building the lead follow-up or intelligence systems for a stronger long-term moat.

## The Five Finalists

### Finalist 1 — AI Content Subscription (GBP + Blog + Social)
**Score: 51 / 60**

The highest-scoring idea because most of the work is already done. The Throttled Up Platform generates GBP posts, blog articles, and social content today. This idea is: package what exists, define the tiers, close clients, and collect $297-497/month each. At 20 clients on the growth tier, that is $10k/month. This is the shortest path to first revenue. The risk is competition — AI content services are not scarce, and clients comparing options will find cheaper alternatives quickly. The counter to that is niche positioning: contractors specifically, not "local businesses generally." Ryan's trades knowledge embedded in the output quality is the differentiation.

### Finalist 2 — AI Local SEO Subscription (Throttled Up Productized)
**Score: 49.5 / 60**

This is Finalist 1 with a higher price point and a fuller service scope. The platform is the same. The difference is positioning: instead of selling content, sell local SEO management at $750-1,500/month. This approach needs 7-14 clients to reach $10k/month instead of 20-34. The trade-off is sales difficulty. A $1,500/month sale requires more trust and proof than a $297/month sale. Contractors buying at this price want case studies, specific rank improvement claims, and a clear accountability structure. The founder dependence concern is real: early clients at this tier often signed because of Ryan personally. The platform needs to prove it can retain clients who signed for the platform, not for Ryan.

### Finalist 3 — AI Lead Follow-Up Service for Contractors (GHL-Based)
**Score: 47.5 / 60**

This idea solves a problem contractors understand viscerally: they are losing jobs to competitors who pick up the phone faster. A 60-second AI response at 11pm beats a call back the next morning every time. The GHL infrastructure exists. The automation is real. The gaps are the AI conversation logic for trades (not trivial to get right) and per-client setup time (1-2 hours each until a VA process is documented). At $297-497/month plus a $500 setup fee, this is competitive with Finalist 1 on revenue math and stronger on stickiness — contractors who see the system booking appointments will not cancel. The primary build risk is the conversation logic breaking on edge cases and generating a bad client experience.

### Finalist 4 — AI YouTube Script + Short-Form Video System for Contractors
**Score: 47 / 60**

The most genuinely passive idea once built. AI writes scripts from a client intake. No platform to maintain beyond prompt quality. No GBP access, no SEO monitoring, no ongoing delivery complexity. The problem is volume: $10k/month requires 26 clients at $397 or 51 at $197. Selling to that many contractors without a distribution channel — either Ryan's existing audience or paid ads — means this is the hardest to grow despite being the easiest to operate. The founder advantage is lower here because Ryan's edge comes from trades knowledge, not from video strategy expertise. Contractors who buy this still have to film the video themselves, which creates a churn driver: they pay for scripts but do not use them.

### Finalist 5 — AI Competitive Intelligence Reports for Contractors
**Score: 45 / 60**

The most defensible idea because no one else is doing it specifically for local contractors at this price point. A monthly report showing what 3-5 competitors are doing — rankings, reviews, ads, content — with specific action recommendations is genuinely useful to a contractor spending $1k-5k/month on marketing. The moat is Ryan's ability to frame what matters: a data dump is worthless, but "your top competitor added 12 reviews last month and is now running ads on three keywords you rank for organically" is actionable. The build requirement is higher than Finalists 1 or 2 (data pipeline is real engineering), and C3 scored 4 rather than 5 because a demo report takes a week to build before the first sale.

## Core Trade-Off for Each Finalist

**Content Subscription:** Speed vs. defensibility. Launch this week, but compete in a crowded market without a strong moat. Niche positioning (contractors only, trades-specific voice) is the only durable differentiator.

**Local SEO Subscription:** Revenue per client vs. sales difficulty. Fewer clients needed for $10k/month, but each sale takes longer to close and depends more on Ryan's personal credibility. Dependence on Ryan is the ceiling.

**Lead Follow-Up Service:** Stickiness vs. setup complexity. Clients who see the system working will not cancel. Getting the system working for each client correctly takes more upfront effort than the content subscription and requires solid AI conversation logic before it is safe to sell at scale.

**Video Scripts:** Operational simplicity vs. growth difficulty. The easiest to run is the hardest to grow. No platform, no ongoing delivery headaches, but also no natural distribution channel. Client churn from non-use (they pay but do not film) is a structural problem.

**Competitive Intelligence:** Defensibility vs. build time. The most differentiated product on the list. Competitors cannot easily replicate it. But the data pipeline is real engineering, and the insight framing requires Ryan's expertise embedded properly in prompts before the product earns its first dollar.

## Bundle Opportunities

**Finalists 1 and 2 are the same platform.** The content subscription and the local SEO subscription both run on the Throttled Up Platform. Ryan does not need to choose between them — he can sell content at $297 as an entry-level offer and full local SEO at $1,500 as the premium tier. Clients who see results on content naturally upgrade. This is a tiered product, not two competing ideas.

**Finalist 3 (Lead Follow-Up) pairs naturally with Finalist 1 or 2.** A contractor who is getting better content and SEO will get more leads. A contractor who gets more leads needs a system to respond to them. Bundling content/SEO with lead follow-up at a combined $597-$797/month is a credible productized offer.

**Finalist 5 (Competitive Intelligence) works as an add-on.** Agencies and higher-spending contractors would pay $247-497/month for competitive monitoring on top of a content or SEO subscription. It is not strong enough as a standalone first product (build time + lower MRR potential), but as a second product layer it adds meaningful revenue per client.

## What Ryan Needs to Decide

This is a choice between two strategies, not fifteen ideas.

**Strategy A — Launch on what exists.**
Start with Finalist 1 (content subscription) or the combined Finalist 1+2 tiered offer. The platform is built. First revenue is possible within two weeks. Use early client feedback to determine whether the lead follow-up service is the right next product to add.

**Strategy B — Build the stronger moat first.**
Invest 3-4 weeks building the lead follow-up system (Finalist 3) before selling. This is more defensible, stickier, and solves a more urgent contractor problem than content. First revenue is slower but retention will be higher.

The two strategies are not mutually exclusive. Content subscription can run while lead follow-up is being built. But starting both simultaneously risks doing neither well.

One additional note: the Done-in-a-Day Sprint (ranked 14th overall) was flagged during research as a valuable discovery tool even though it does not meet the under-5-hours-per-week goal. Running 2-3 sprints before choosing a product direction would generate $3k-$9k in fast revenue and reveal exactly which contractor problems are worth building systems around. It is not the business — it is a way to fund and inform the business before committing to a build.

**The decision Ryan needs to make:**
- Strategy A: launch the content/SEO subscription now on what is already built
- Strategy B: spend 3-4 weeks building the lead follow-up system before selling anything
- Strategy C: run 2-3 implementation sprints first to fund and inform the decision, then build

Ryan should not select a winner today without first answering: which problem do contractors in your existing network complain about most — getting found online, responding to leads, or knowing what competitors are doing?
