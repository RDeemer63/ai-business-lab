# Venture Studio Manifesto

**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** 2026-07-09
**Status:** DRAFT — not in effect until Ryan approves

---

## What This Organization Is

AI Business Lab is an autonomous venture studio. It is not a company, not an agency, and not a consulting practice.

A venture studio discovers, validates, launches, and retires businesses — repeatedly, systematically, and faster with each cycle. The AI employees do the work. Ryan sets the direction, approves decisions at defined gates, and reviews the portfolio.

The product is not any individual business. The product is the studio's ability to repeatedly produce businesses.

---

## What It Produces

Launched, validated businesses — not reports, not content, not deliverables.

A business counts as launched when it has at least one paying client and a delivery system that does not require Ryan's direct involvement to operate.

A business counts as validated when a 7-day test produces a documented go or no-go signal — regardless of which direction the signal points.

---

## What Kinds of Businesses We Build

Every business evaluated or launched by this studio must meet all of these requirements.

**Business model requirements:**
- Recurring revenue — monthly subscription or annual contract, not one-time payments
- AI-deliverable — the core service is produced by AI agents, not by humans
- Operable under 5 hours per week — after the first 90 days, Ryan's total involvement is under 5 hours per week
- Documentable in a single workflow — the delivery process can be written down and handed to an agent

**Market requirements:**
- A specific, painful problem — not a "nice to have," a "must fix"
- An identifiable buyer — a named job title, company type, or situation
- A clear monetization path — the buyer has a budget and a reason to pay recurring fees

**Size requirements:**
- Realistic path to $5k MRR within 90 days of first client
- Ceiling of at least $10k MRR without adding headcount

**Hard exclusions — do not evaluate or build:**
- Requires Ryan's personal reputation to sell
- Requires frequent sales calls to close or retain
- Requires custom fulfillment per client
- Requires Ryan's time to scale past 10 clients
- Requires a skill the studio cannot acquire within 30 days

## What Counts as Success

**Studio-level success (primary):**
Business launch velocity — how many ideas can the studio take from concept to a validated go/no-go result per month.

| Milestone | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Year 1 | 2 ideas validated per month |
| Year 2 | 4 ideas validated per month, 1 business launched per month |
| Long-term | The 12th business launches faster and cheaper than the 1st |

The studio is succeeding when it learns faster than it spends.

**Individual business success (secondary):**
- $5k MRR within 90 days of first client — without Ryan directly delivering the service
- Client retention above 70% at 90 days

**What is not a success metric:**
- Revenue from a business that requires Ryan to function
- A validated idea that never launches
- A launched business that Ryan can't step away from

## When to Kill an Idea

Kill without negotiation when any of these conditions are met:

1. The 7-day validation test produces zero genuine expressions of buying intent
2. Two consecutive validation iterations produce no improvement in signal
3. Ryan's estimated time-to-stabilize exceeds 20 hours
4. The business requires a skill the studio cannot acquire
5. The delivery system cannot be documented in a single workflow after 30 days of operation

Killing an idea is not failure. It is the machine working correctly. Every killed idea produces a knowledge base entry that makes the next evaluation faster.

---

## When to Double Down

Commit additional resources when all three conditions are met:

1. Validation test produces 3 or more genuine expressions of buying intent within 7 days
2. At least one prospect is willing to pay before the product fully exists
3. The delivery process can be outlined in a workflow before the first client signs

One condition is encouraging. Two is interesting. All three is a green light.

## The Autonomy Ladder

The studio moves through phases of autonomy over time. The current phase is always documented in NEXT_ACTIONS.md.

| Phase | AI does | Ryan does |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| 1 | Researches | Decides |
| 2 | Researches and recommends | Approves |
| 3 | Researches, recommends, and executes | Reviews results |
| 4 | Runs full launch cycles | Reviews portfolio monthly |

**Target at 12 months:** Phase 3 for research and validation. Phase 2 for all client-facing work.

We do not skip phases. Phase 3 only begins after Phase 2 has produced documented, reliable results. Phase 4 is earned, not assumed.

---

## Decisions That Always Require Ryan

No agent may take the following actions without a Needs Ryan approval in NEXT_ACTIONS.md, regardless of phase:

- Send any external communication (email, message, post)
- Make any financial commitment
- Select a business model to build
- Sign or represent any client contract
- Kill a business that has paying clients
- Add a new department or permanently expand the core team

These gates do not change as the studio matures. They are permanent.

## Operating Principles

This manifesto inherits all principles from `00-command-center/OPERATING_PRINCIPLES.md`.

Two additional principles introduced by the venture studio model:

**12. Launch velocity over perfection.** A business validated quickly and correctly is worth more than a business validated slowly and completely. The goal is signal, not certainty. Two validated ideas per month at 80% confidence beats one validated idea per month at 95% confidence.

**13. The portfolio absorbs failure.** Individual businesses will fail. That is expected and accounted for. The studio succeeds when the portfolio as a whole grows. Do not treat individual failures as organizational failures. Treat them as tuition.

## What This Is Not

This is not a side project. A side project is abandoned when life gets busy.

This is not an experiment. An experiment has no operational discipline.

This is not a content business. Content is a distribution channel, not the product.

This is not a consulting practice. Consulting scales with Ryan's time. This studio does not.

This is an organization. It has departments, agents, workflows, institutional memory, and a mandate to improve itself over time. It runs whether Ryan is paying attention or not — except at the defined human gates.
