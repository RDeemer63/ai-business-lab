# Investment Criteria

What makes a business worth pursuing. A business must clear all criteria in each section to advance to the build phase.

These criteria are inherited from the Venture Studio Manifesto and expanded with additional detail. The Manifesto is the canonical source. This file adds the reasoning behind each criterion.

---

## Business model requirements

**Recurring revenue**
Monthly subscription or annual contract. One-time payments are excluded because they require constant new customer acquisition to maintain revenue. The studio needs businesses that compound.

**AI-deliverable core service**
The primary value delivered to clients is produced by AI agents. Ryan's involvement in delivery is at the oversight level, not the execution level. A business that requires Ryan to personally produce the output is a job, not a business.

**Under 5 hours per week after 90 days**
After the first 90 days of operation, Ryan's total time involvement is under 5 hours per week. This is a hard ceiling, not a soft target. If a business cannot reach this threshold, it will prevent the studio from launching the next one.

**Documentable in a single workflow**
The delivery process can be written down completely in one workflow file. If it cannot be documented clearly enough for an agent to execute it, it cannot be systematized. If it cannot be systematized, it will always require Ryan's judgment.

---

## Market requirements

**A specific, painful problem**
Not "nice to have," not "would be helpful," but a problem the buyer is actively trying to solve or failing to solve. Pain creates urgency. Urgency creates willingness to pay.

**An identifiable buyer**
A named job title, company type, or situation. "Small businesses" is not an identifiable buyer. "HVAC contractors with 3-10 trucks who are losing Google leads to competitors" is an identifiable buyer. The more specific the buyer, the easier the validation test.

**A clear monetization path**
The buyer has a defined budget for this type of problem and a reason to pay recurring fees. This means either: (a) they are already paying someone else to solve this problem, or (b) the cost of not solving it is measurable and exceeds the price.

---

## Size requirements

**Path to $5k MRR within 90 days of first client**
Not 90 days from now. 90 days from when the first paying client signs. This requires knowing the price point and the acquisition path.

**Ceiling of at least $10k MRR without adding headcount**
The business model must be able to scale to $10k MRR with the same agent infrastructure. If scaling requires adding human staff, it is not the right model for this studio.

---

## Hard exclusions

No evaluation or build if any of these are true:

- Requires Ryan's personal reputation to sell
- Requires frequent sales calls to close or retain clients
- Requires custom fulfillment per client
- Requires Ryan's time to scale past 10 clients
- Requires a skill the studio cannot acquire within 30 days

These exclusions are not ranked by severity. Any single one is disqualifying.

---

## How to use this document

When an agent recommends advancing a business idea, it should cite which criteria the idea meets and flag any criteria that are uncertain. Ryan evaluates uncertain criteria. If a criterion cannot be evaluated without a test, the next step is to design an experiment to test it -- not to assume the answer.

Criteria changes require Ryan's approval and a version note at the bottom of this file.
